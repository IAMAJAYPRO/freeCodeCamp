Creamy Chocolate Fudge

Description

Fudge is a dense, rich confection typically made with sugar, milk or cream, butter and chocolate or other flavorings.

The base for fudge is boiled until it reaches the soft-ball stage (135 to 140 degrees F), then stirred or beaten as it cools to minimize the formation of sugar crystals.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups white sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter (1.5 sticks)
  • 2/3 cup evaporated milk
  • 12 oz. semisweet chocolate chips (can substitute other kinds of chips to make different flavors of fudge, like butterscotch, peanut butter, etc.)
  • One 7 oz. jar marshmallow cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • (Optional) Additional extracts like caramel or peppermint
  • (Optional) Toppings like toffee bits, crushed candy canes, or Andes mints
  • (Optional) 1 cup chopped walnuts

Steps

  1. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. In a large saucepan over medium heat, combine sugar, butter, and evaporated milk. Stirring constantly, bring to a full boil and cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in chocolate chips until melted and mixture is smooth.
  4. Fold in marshmallow cream and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. (Optional) Stir in additional extracts, if desired, for different flavor variations.
  6. Pour mixture into prepared baking dish, spreading evenly.
  7. (Optional) Sprinkle with toppings like toffee bits, crushed candy canes, or Andes mints, if desired.
  8. Let cool at room temperature until set, then cut into squares.
